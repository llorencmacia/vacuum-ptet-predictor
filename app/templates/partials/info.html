Technical Documentation

This application computes the Pressure Total Evacuation Time (PTET) based on the following differential formulation:

$$ \frac{-rT}{V} \, dt = \frac{dp}{\dot{m}_s(p)\, n(p)} $$

Ejector Characteristic Curves

The secondary mass flow rate is modeled as a piecewise linear function with a defined breakpoint:

$$ \dot{m}_s (p) = \begin{cases} a + b \cdot p & \text{if } p \geq p_{bP} \\ c + d \cdot p & \text{if } p < p_{bP} \end{cases} $$

The user provides the maximum mass flow rate, the breakpoint pressure, and the slope of the first segment. The remaining coefficients are constructed to ensure continuity. The pressure-dependent polytropic exponent curve is defined as:

$$ n(p) = e + f \cdot p + g \cdot p^2 $$

PTET Calculation

The total discharge time is obtained by integrating the governing equation across both pressure regions:

$$ \text{PTET} = \frac{-V}{rT} \left[ I_1 + I_2 \right] $$

where:

$$ I_1 = \int_{p_{atm}}^{p_{bP}} \frac{dp}{(a + b p)(e + f p + g p^2)} ; I_2 = \int_{p_{bP}}^{p_{min}} \frac{dp}{(c + d p)(e + f p + g p^2)} $$ $$ $$
Note: The calculation requires the tank volume (V), specific gas constant (r), operating temperature (T), ejector characteristic parameters, and polytropic coefficients.
